Nupoor Gandhi

I am a third-year PhD student in Machine Learning and Public Policy at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Emma Strubell. I am primarily interested in extracting structured meaning representations from text in low-resource, policy-relevant domains. Previously, I received my bachelors in Computer Science with a minor in Math at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. At Illinois I worked on modeling public health outcomes using social media data as a part of the Text Information Management and Analysis Group and the Health and Social Media Group working with ChengXiang Zhai and Dolores Albarracin. I was also a Data Science for Social Good Fellow at Imperial College London, where I worked on a QA system for nonprofit Barefoot Law to accelerate access to legal advice.
